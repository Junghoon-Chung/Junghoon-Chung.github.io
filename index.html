Who I
Am

Human · Data · Systems

I'm a PhD candidate in Industrial and Systems Engineering at the University of Washington, advised by Dr. Ji-Eun Kim in the HAS Lab. My research sits at the intersection of human factors, machine learning, and behavioral science.

I focus on predicting and understanding human performance through complex multimodal data — physiological signals, behavioral logs, and contextual cues — to build adaptive systems that meet people where they are, not the other way around.

Previously at Virginia Tech's OEB Lab, I built end-to-end data science pipelines for longitudinal workplace studies, published at HFES 2025, and completed an M.S. thesis on sit-stand desk usage patterns among knowledge workers.

Research & Projects
03
University of Washington · Current
Human Vigilance Modeling with Causal Inference
Multimodal physiological study predicting vigilance and alertness in safety-critical environments using ML and causal inference.

Investigated how cognitive load and sustained attention degrade in safety-critical environments. Designed controlled studies capturing user states through physiological signals (ECG, EDA, eye tracking). Generated actionable insights for designing systems that support attention and reduce cognitive overload. P.I: Dr. Ji-Eun Kim.

Built predictive models of moment-to-moment alertness using multimodal physiological time-series data. Engineered 30+ features across 10,000+ observations per participant. Applied causal inference to identify key drivers of performance variability. Achieved 20% accuracy improvement via feature engineering and model tuning. Stack: Python, scikit-learn, PyTorch.

Virginia Tech
Context-Aware Sit-Stand Intervention
End-to-end research pipeline on knowledge worker posture behavior, combining field observations with longitudinal sensor data.

Led mixed-methods research on knowledge worker interactions with sit-stand desks. Conducted interviews, surveys, and behavioral observations. Synthesized qualitative feedback with sensor data to identify usability barriers and contextual behavior drivers. Published at HFES 2025.

End-to-end pipeline to predict posture transitions from 3+ weeks of longitudinal data (100k+ records/participant). Implemented zero-shot NLP classification, time-series forecasting with contextual variables, and clustering-based personalization. Stack: Python, HuggingFace, scikit-learn.

Virginia Tech
Human-Robot-Remote Worker Collaboration
User study on human-robot-remote worker collaboration capturing EMG, EEG, IMU, and eye-tracking data.

User study on collaborative dynamics between co-located humans, robots, and remote workers. Collected subjective questionnaires and behavioral observations to understand workload, comfort, and ergonomic outcomes in hybrid environments.

Captured and processed multimodal data: EMG, EEG, IMU, and eye-tracking. Performed time-series analysis of posture logs and applied statistical hypothesis testing to validate physical and cognitive workload hypotheses.

Yonsei University
Review Reliability via BERT & Clustering
Large-scale product review bias analysis using BERT sentiment modeling and clustering across 50,000+ reviews.

Investigated how reviewer bias distorts product ratings, undermining user trust. Proposed cluster-normalized scoring grounded in behavioral segmentation to improve fairness and reliability of large-scale review systems.

Analyzed 50,000+ reviews using BERT sentiment modeling and k-means clustering. Identified systematic bias patterns across reviewer groups. Developed cluster-normalized scoring to reduce bias in aggregate ratings.

Virginia Tech
AI-Based Biomechanical Exposure Assessment
De-biased AI-based biomechanical exposure assessment using 3D motion capture and physiological data collection.

Designed controlled experiments capturing diverse participant demographics. Focused on responsible, de-biased AI to ensure biomechanical risk assessments work equitably across different body types and motion profiles. P.I: Dr. Sol Lim.

Collected and analyzed 3D motion capture and physiological data to build a dataset for de-biased AI-based biomechanical exposure assessment. Applied preprocessing pipelines to ensure diverse, high-quality training data.
